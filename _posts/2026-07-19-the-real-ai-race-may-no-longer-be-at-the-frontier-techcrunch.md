The focus of the AI race is shifting from the most powerful models to the practical use of open-weight models.

Data shows that while frontier models capture the headlines, open-weight models are increasingly powering the infrastructure of AI applications. For example, Chinese open-weight models accounted for 41% of downloads on Hugging Face this spring. Developers are turning to these models because they are often cheaper and easier to customize than premium, closed-source APIs. Many organizations are now using open models to handle the high-volume requests that form the bulk of their production workloads.

This shift matters for the public sector and large enterprises that need to prioritize data governance and cost control. Relying on a single provider's API can create vendor lock-in and limit visibility into how data is processed or used for training. By choosing open-weight models, public institutions can host their own AI infrastructure and keep their core capabilities private. This approach allows for more transparency, making it easier to identify and patch security risks. It also helps prevent the concentration of technology in the hands of a few large companies, which can create an asymmetry of power.

Does your organization prioritize the convenience of a closed API or the control of an open-weight model?
