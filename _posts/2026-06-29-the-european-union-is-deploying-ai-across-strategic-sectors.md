The European Union is moving from high-level AI policy to practical deployment across several key industries.

A new report by the OECD and the European AI Office examines how AI is being used in agriculture, healthcare, manufacturing, and mobility. It highlights specific use cases, such as AI-assisted medical imaging, predictive maintenance in factories, and precision tools for sustainable farming.

This matters because it identifies the practical barriers to scaling these technologies. For those working in the public sector, the focus shifts to solving issues like fragmented data, limited rural infrastructure, and the need for clear evidence on how these tools actually improve public services.
