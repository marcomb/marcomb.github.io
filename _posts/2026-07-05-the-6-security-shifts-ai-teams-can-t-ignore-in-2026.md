AI security is shifting as systems move from simple tools to autonomous agents capable of taking direct actions in enterprise environments.

The article covers how these "agentic" systems create new risks, such as the rise of non-human identities (NHIs) and prompt injection. It highlights that because AI agents often have access to internal databases, they require their own unique credentials, real-time monitoring, and "circuit breakers" to prevent them from being hijacked by attackers.

For the public sector, these shifts are critical for data governance and infrastructure safety. Protecting sensitive citizen data requires moving toward a zero-trust model where every AI interaction is verified. Implementing strict oversight and human-led code reviews can help ensure that automated systems remain secure and compliant with public service standards.

Are your security protocols ready to manage an environment where machine identities may soon outnumber human ones?
