The EU AI Act is moving toward practical implementation with new transparency tools and updated timelines.

The European Parliament recently approved measures to simplify the Act. This includes new deadlines for high-risk systems and a ban on "nudifier" apps. The Commission also introduced official icons for labeling AI-generated content and published FAQs for the Transparency Code of Practice. These tools help organizations understand how to meet disclosure requirements for deepfakes and other AI content.

For AI practitioners and public sector leaders, these updates highlight the transition from theory to practice. There is an ongoing discussion about whether pre-market checklists are enough to manage risk. Many experts suggest that robust monitoring and clear liability frameworks are also necessary to protect public safety without slowing down innovation. The GDPR also remains a key tool for addressing harms in cases where the AI Act may not apply.

How do you plan to use these new transparency tools in your organization?
