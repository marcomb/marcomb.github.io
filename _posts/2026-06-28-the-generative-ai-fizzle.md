AI market valuations may be entering a period of slow decline rather than a sudden crash.

The concept of a "Generative AI Fizzle" suggests that the gap between high hype and actual profits is starting to shrink. Large language models (LLMs) are increasingly becoming commodities, which leads to price wars and makes it harder for providers to maintain high profit margins.

For those in the public sector, this shift is worth noting. It suggests a move away from chasing the newest hype and toward finding sustainable, cost-effective solutions. As the market stabilizes, understanding the true costs and long-term viability of AI tools becomes more important for public projects.

Is your organization prioritizing long-term utility over current market trends?
