Here is a project that monitors how big tech companies change their corporate policies using AI.

The tool, called PolicyWatcher, tracks privacy policies, terms of service, and AI governance rules for 16 large companies. It uses a scraper to detect changes in these documents and then applies Google Gemini to analyze those updates. The system provides a bilingual analysis in English and Italian, focusing on risk factors and regional impacts across the EU and the US. It also evaluates these changes against a 15-KPI governance framework. This helps users see how corporate behavior shifts over time. The platform includes features like a "freshness guard" to ensure data is current and a "KPI preservation" system to maintain consistent governance metrics during new scans.

For the public sector and compliance professionals, this tool simplifies the task of monitoring how large entities handle data and AI ethics. It turns dense, technical legal documents into clear, actionable intelligence. This is useful for regulators who need to track corporate adherence to standards or for SMEs trying to understand the risks of using new AI services. It provides a transparent way to see how policy changes might affect different jurisdictions. The developers designed it as a civic tech tool to make complex information accessible to a wider audience.
