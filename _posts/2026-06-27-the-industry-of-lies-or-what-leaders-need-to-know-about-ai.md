AI adoption involves practical challenges that go beyond the technology itself.

The reality of implementing AI often includes unpredictable costs and technical hurdles. Many organizations face issues with usage-based billing, where reasoning tokens and large context windows can make budgets difficult to forecast. There is also a risk of "shadow AI," where employees use personal accounts to bypass company limits, potentially exposing sensitive data.

For those in the public sector, these points are critical for responsible governance. It is important to move past the hype and focus on realistic budgeting and data security. Diversifying your AI stack and using open-weight models for simpler tasks can help control costs while maintaining high standards for data protection.

How is your organization planning for these unpredictable AI costs?
