New research highlights how artificial intelligence is being used to monitor and respond to global public health threats.

The report from the EC Apply AI Alliance examines the specific ways machine learning helps identify disease outbreaks and track biological risks. It explores how AI can analyze vast amounts of data from various sources, such as social media, clinical records, and environmental sensors, to find patterns that might escape human observation. These tools help health authorities respond to emergencies more quickly by predicting where a threat might spread next. The report also looks at how these technologies can support faster drug discovery and more accurate vaccine distribution across different regions.

For those working in the public sector, this development shows the importance of managing dual-use technologies. While AI offers a way to improve public service efficiency and protect citizens, it also creates risks regarding biosecurity and the potential for misuse. Practitioners must think about how to build governance frameworks that allow for innovation while maintaining strict safety standards. This involves creating clear rules for data privacy, ensuring transparency in how models make decisions, and establishing accountability for the outputs. Ensuring that these tools are used responsibly is a key priority for European health security and maintaining public trust.

How should European institutions balance the need for rapid AI adoption with these specific security risks?
