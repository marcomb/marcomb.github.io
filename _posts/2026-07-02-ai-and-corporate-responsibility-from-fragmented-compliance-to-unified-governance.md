The EU AI Act is shifting the focus from voluntary AI ethics to mandatory corporate governance.

The text explores the "governance gap" created by AI's ability to impact employment, healthcare, and social services. It explains how the Fundamental Rights Impact Assessment (FRIA) requires companies to evaluate how high-risk systems affect human rights as a legal requirement.

For AI practitioners and public sector leaders, this means rights protection must be built into the system design. It moves human rights from a voluntary goal to a core compliance task, similar to environmental or supply chain standards. This shift suggests that EU standards may soon set the benchmark for global AI governance.

Is your team ready for these mandatory impact assessments?
