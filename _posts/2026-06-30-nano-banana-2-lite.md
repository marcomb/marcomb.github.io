Google released Nano Banana 2 Lite, a new image model designed for speed and cost efficiency.

The model, also known as Gemini 3.1 Flash Lite Image, focuses on high-velocity image generation. Recent tests show it can handle detailed prompts well, though it still produces occasional spelling errors in text within the images.

This matters for the public sector because "Lite" models offer a way to scale visual content without high operational costs. They are practical for large-scale projects, but the current limitations mean human review is still essential before using these images in official communications.

How do you balance the need for scale with the need for accuracy in public projects?
