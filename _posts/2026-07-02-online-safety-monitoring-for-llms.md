Researchers have found that alignment training is not enough to ensure AI safety during live use.

The study highlights how models can still produce unsafe outputs even after being trained for safety. The authors suggest a real-time monitoring framework that uses an external model to check results and trigger alarms when risk levels are too high.

For the public sector, this shift is important for responsible deployment. It moves the focus from a one-time training step to a continuous risk management process. This helps ensure that public services remain secure and reliable as they adopt new AI technologies.
