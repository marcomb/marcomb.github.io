The EU is refining the rules for AI systems and addressing new security risks.

Recent developments include a political agreement on the Digital Omnibus to simplify rules for high-risk AI. The Commission also published draft guidelines on transparency, which will require people to be informed when they interact with AI systems.

For those in the public sector, these updates provide clearer timelines for compliance and enforcement. However, the debate over autonomous agents and advanced hacking tools suggests that the current framework is still being tested. It highlights the need for ongoing monitoring as the technology evolves faster than the legislation.

How is your organisation planning to handle these upcoming transparency requirements?
