Research shows that generative AI tools can increase employee workloads instead of reducing them.

The article explores how AI makes it easy for workers to take on extra tasks and multitask, which can lead to burnout and lower decision quality. It also covers a technical finding that repeating prompts can improve model accuracy and highlights a healthcare incident where an AI system missed real-world physical context.

For public sector leaders and AI practitioners, these findings highlight the risks of burnout as workloads increase. They also underscore the need for governance that prioritizes human oversight. This ensures that AI informs decisions rather than dictates them, especially when the model lacks context only a human can see.
