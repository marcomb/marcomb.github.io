The AI industry is moving from a period of initial excitement to a phase of practical reality.

Recent data shows that large companies are cutting back on AI spending as costs rise and reliability remains inconsistent. While some models can solve complex problems, they often struggle with basic, everyday tasks. This highlights a significant gap between what the technology can do in a lab and how it performs in a real production environment.

For the public sector, this shift is a key takeaway. Success depends on moving beyond impressive demos to find tools that offer stable returns and consistent accuracy. Real transformation happens when we bridge the gap between high-end capabilities and the reliable performance needed for public services.

How is your organization balancing AI costs with the need for reliable results?
