Pope Leo XIV has released "Magnifica Humanitas," a significant new encyclical on AI governance.

The document outlines moral principles for AI, focusing on transparency, the dignity of work, and the need for human oversight in automated decisions. It specifically calls for systems to be understandable and contestable, arguing that AI should not replace moral judgment.

For AI practitioners and public sector leaders, this matters because it provides a moral framework that can influence policy debates and public perception in many European countries. It highlights a growing demand for human-in-the-loop requirements in healthcare and social services, which may change how organizations approach procurement and risk management.
