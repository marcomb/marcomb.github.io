Software engineers are finding it harder to manage the influx of low-quality code generated by AI.

The discussion highlights a growing divide between those who use AI to produce quick results and the experienced engineers who must fix those results. This "workslop" creates an illusion of productivity while actually increasing the workload for senior staff who have to debug and maintain the output.

For the public sector, this trend is worth noting regarding long-term maintenance. If AI-generated code is used to build public infrastructure without strict quality controls, it can lead to significant technical debt. Ensuring that AI tools improve efficiency without compromising system stability is a key challenge for IT leaders.
