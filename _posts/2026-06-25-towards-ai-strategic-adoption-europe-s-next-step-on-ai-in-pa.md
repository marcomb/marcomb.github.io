Europe is moving from testing AI tools to integrating them into the core functions of public administration.

A recent brief from the European Commission’s Joint Research Centre explains how to move past isolated pilots. It suggests a framework based on three pillars: anchoring AI in European values, adapting internal infrastructure and skills, and applying it to high-impact use cases.

This is important because public administrations do more than just use AI; they shape how the technology spreads across society. By focusing on strategic integration rather than just technology, governments can build public trust and improve services for citizens and businesses.
