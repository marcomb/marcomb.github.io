Companies are beginning to limit how much AI tokens employees use for basic tasks.

Many organizations initially encouraged high AI usage to see what was possible. Now, firms are noticing that small, repetitive requests—like converting files into slides—are consuming significant budgets without offering much value. Some companies are moving toward "token rationing" to manage these costs more effectively.

For AI practitioners and the public sector, this shift highlights the importance of cost governance. It is no longer just about whether the technology works, but whether it provides a clear return on investment. Ensuring that public funds are used for high-impact projects rather than trivial tasks will be a key focus for leadership.
