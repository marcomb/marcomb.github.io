The  ISO/IEC 42001 standard provides a framework for organizations to manage AI risks, documentation, and human oversight. It is the first global standard for AI management systems and helps companies organize policies, roles, and monitoring records. While it does not replace the legal requirements of the AI Act, it offers a certifiable management system that provides auditable evidence of responsible AI use. Major technology providers like AWS, Microsoft, and Anthropic have already begun seeking these certifications to provide assurance to their customers.

For AI practitioners and the public sector, this matters because it translates complex legal obligations into practical workflows. The EU AI Act requires structured evidence for high-risk systems, particularly in areas like education, healthcare, and public services. Using a recognized standard helps organizations establish clear processes for data quality, cybersecurity, and incident reporting. It also helps procurement teams verify that vendors are managing risks effectively. For leaders, this approach helps mitigate the risk of significant fines while building trust with citizens and stakeholders.
